    • Single-pixel Imaging System Using End-to-end Time Series Inspired Neural Network

    • Songlin Xu, Yue Chen, Yanding Peng, Liqun He and Rongde Lu

    • Will be submitted to IEEE Signal Processing Letters(Excellent Project Award in Student Innovation Program of USTC)

    Index Terms 1: Optics, Image Processing
    Index Terms 2: Single Pixel Camera, Deep Learning, Digital Image Processing

    @ Student innovation program at USTC      Advisor: Prof. Rongde Lu

        Single-pixel camera is a new kind of imaging system which can be used into reducing the cost of high resolution imaging and detecting objects beyond the visible wavelength of light according to compress sensing theory. Whereas a trade-off problem is the balance between acquisition time and reconstruction time. Because less sampling means more recovery time. Based on this, a novel time series inspired end to-end neural network combining convolutional neural nerwork and long short-term memory neural network is proposed to reduce the recovery time with less sampling in this paper. Our end-to-end neural network can recover images of different sizes within the recovery time of less than 0.5 second from a singlepixel camera sampling at a compression ratio of less than 0.1% in the experiment. The experiment shows that our method needs less recovery time than the state-of-art methods. Besides, our method can achieve relatively high PSNR(peak signal-to-noise ratio) and SSIM(structural similarity index) under much less sampling than other methods.
